Testing: Cut — slice a panel along parallel planes

Cut takes a composed panel and splits it into N slices along parallel cut planes. Three parameters drive the geometry: rip (plane rotation about Y, in degrees), bevel (plane tilt from vertical, 45–90°), and either pitch (plane spacing in mm) or slices (desired slice count — pitch is then derived from the safe extent). Override any of them via URL: ?rip=30&bevel=60&slices=6.

Expected in each tile:

  • Input — the composed maple + walnut panel (100×50×400 mm by default), rendered top-down.
  • Operation — two ortho projections. TOP: dashed cut lines at the rip angle, triangular offcut regions dimmed at each end. SIDE: the same cuts viewed from +X as elevation lines, tilted from vertical by (90° − bevel).
  • Output — the N surviving slices in 3D, laid out along Z at their original cut angle with a uniform gap so every interior cut face is visible; ghosted offcuts at the ends. Free orbit, home button, and an XYZ axis gizmo sit in the upper-right.
